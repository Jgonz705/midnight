Gamecube Console Repair

Common issue #1

Console will not boot

#1 Check power cord - First, remove the power cord from the back of Gamecube and check for any exposure of the wire. If exposed, it's likely the issue relates to the power cord and it's best to replace the cord after exhausting further steps ahead. It's also likely that the port in which the power cord is inserted may have dust that is blocking the power cord.                                                                                                                                      #2 Check for overheating - Unplug the gamecube from any wires that may be connected and look for the grated grid on the console. Get an air compressor or gently blow on the grated grid to clear up any dust that may be blocking airflow from cooling the gamecube. Next, plug the powercord in and observe the grid and check if the fan inside spins, if so then it's likely the issue is not the air flow and the issue persists somewhere else. If the fan doesn't spin, you're likely to have to open the console and check if anything is obsuring the fan.

Gamecube Console Repair

Common issue #2

Console can't read disks

#1 Check for working game disc- If you're not certain if the console can read discs. I'd recommened the simple solution of making sure the game disc being used is perfectly functioning. You can inspect the discs for any notable large scratches or perhaps using a different Gamecube at a local retro store.                                                                            #2 Replacing the optical laser - if you've already made sure that the game disc is working however the gamecube still won't read, it's likely that the laser used to read the discs is not aligned or simply does not function anymore. This process is a rather complex one and requires a phillips screwdriver in order to open the gamecube. Once opened, you want to seperate the optical drive asssembly from the gamecube and work with the optical drive assembly to gently dissassemble it. Once you've taken it apart, you may find the optical laser inside of the optical drive and you may replace this part using parts from another gamecube or ordering a brand new part online.

Gamecube Controller Repair

Common issue

Loose joysticks/sticky buttons

#1 Open the controller - This is a rather simple and easy fix with the most difficult part being is having the right tool. The tool necessary to open a Gamecube controller is a tri-wing screwdriver which can be found at many tool stores or Walmart and is used to open many electronics. Once you've obtained you're tri-wing screwdriver simply use it to take apart the controller and make sure the buttons and sticks are aligned properly. in most cases, taking it apart and putting it back together will elimate the issue however if it still persists than you may order additional joysticks and buttons for the controller online and simply replace your old sticks.                                                                                                 #2 Clean the controller- If you're experiencing the buttons becoming stuck or being sticky, it's very likely that the issue is that the Controller is dirty. Even if the controller doesn't look visually dirty, it's likely that there is dirt underneath the buttons that you're not able to see on the outside. Similar to solution #1, you should open the controller and grab a wipe and slowly go over each button untill it's fully cleaned. you may also use soap and water as well as a toothbrush to ensure that the buttons are clean and that will very likely solve the issue.
