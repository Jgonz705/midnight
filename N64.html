N64 Console Repair

Plugged in but can't see screen

Check the ports - A very common issue that is due to our advancements in technology. Due to the Nintendo 64's age, it hasn't adapted too well to modern televisions that have shifted towards an HDMI picture output. The first thing I'd inspect is if there is any sound coming out of the television when plugging the TV in. If so, then it's likely the order in which the (Red, Yellow, White) video ports are out of order; try to simply change the order in which you plug them in and try to match the colors you plug them in.                           #2 Insert game before boot up - Although this may seem strange, it's very possible that the reason you're not seeing anything on the screen is because the Nintendo 64 hasn't boot up the game. This is an issue that relates again, to the age of the N64 and it requires that you boot up the console in specific order of steps. 1. Turn off N64. 2. Place game cartridge in (blow before inserting) 3.Turn N64 back on.

N64 controller repair

Joystick is 'slow' or 'sticks'

Clean the inside of the controller - If your joystick is sluggish, then it's really showing its battle scars. In order to fix this issue, get a phillips screwdriver and unscrew the three screws in the back. Set the buttons to the side and proceed by removing the black screw in the back and gently open the controller and carefully remove all the axis bases. proceed by cleaning the motherboard with a cotton swab and a small amount of alcohol then take your time to clean the buttons. You may place the buttons in a container that has alchohol in it and let them sit in the alcohol or you may clean them by hand. Now once you've cleaned the main parts, you may put the controller back together and now you'll have a controller that looks and feels brand new.
